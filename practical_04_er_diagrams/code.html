Viva Questions & Answers

1. What is the difference between Strong and Weak entities?

expand_more
A strong entity exists independently and has its own primary key. A weak entity depends on a identifying owner entity and uses a partial key (discriminator) combined with the owner's primary key for identification.

2. Define Participation Constraints in E-R Modeling.

expand_more
Participation constraints specify whether the existence of an entity depends on its being related to another entity via the relationship set. It can be Total (Double line) or Partial (Single line).

3. When should we use EER (Enhanced ER) diagrams over standard ER?

expand_more
EER is used when we need to model complex requirements like Specialization, Generalization, and Aggregation, which are not easily captured by standard ER modeling.
